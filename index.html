Administrative Zones

  • Aba Zone
  • Ohafia Zone
  • Osisioma Zone
  • Umuahia Zone

Aba Zone

Aba Zone comprises Aba North, Aba South, Isiala Ngwa North, Isiala Ngwa South, and Obingwa LGAs. It is known for its vibrant commercial activities, including markets, manufacturing businesses, and private enterprises. This zone focuses on placing corps members in industries, private firms, and commercial establishments, as well as executing community service projects aimed at enhancing local commerce and industry.

Ohafia Zone

Ohafia Zone comprises Ohafia, Arochukwu, Isuikwuato, and LGAs. It is known for its rich cultural heritage and agricultural activities.

This zone places corps members in rural schools, health centers, and agricultural projects, emphasizing community development and cultural integration activities.

Osisioma Zone

Osisioma Zone comprises Osisioma Ngwa, Ugwunagbo, Ukwa East, and Ukwa West LGAs. It is an area with a mix of urban and rural characteristics, focusing on developing infrastructure and community projects. This zone places corps members in various sectors, including education, healthcare, and community development initiatives, aiming to improve the living standards and infrastructure within the LGAs.

Umuahia Zone

Umuahia Zone comprises Umuahia North, Umuahia South, Ikwuano, Bende, and Umunneochi LGAs. As the capital city of Abia State, Umuahia serves as a major administrative center with numerous government offices, educational institutions, and healthcare facilities. This zone coordinates corps members' activities, placing them in ministries, schools, and hospitals, and handles logistics related to corps members' welfare and community development projects.
